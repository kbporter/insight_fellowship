Recommendations:

Since that the time taken to respond and the accuracy of the responses are the most influential features in the model, Constant Therapy may want to adjust the difficulty of their starting tasks. Drop-off users perform worse on average than active users, suggesting that the tasks assigned to new users are too difficult, potentially causing frustration with the app. Furthermore, prompting users to slow down after an inaccurate response may facilitate their experience.

The level of difficulty and task type also seem to affect the two groups differently. Drop-off users perform worse than active users on Task B, but better on task A. Decreasing the starting difficulty of Task B, and increasing the starting difficulty of Task A is recommended.

The first two levels of difficulty also seem to not be optimized. Fewer drop-off users perform well on the least difficult tasks, but those assigned the second level of difficulty perform better than the active users on average.

Additionally, it seems as though the platform may influence drop-off matching the features across the two platforms may increase user retention on the less supported platform
